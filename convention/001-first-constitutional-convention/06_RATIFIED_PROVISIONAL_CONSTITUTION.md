# Ratified Provisional Constitution (Scaffolding)

## Article 1: Purpose, Identity, and Non-Goals

> Anarchadia is constituted as a local-first, offline-first digital toolkit for self-governing groups and federated commons. It provides primitives for direct-democratic decision-making, local resource stewardship, and personal opacity. It explicitly rejects the premature deployment of global planning authorities, bare-metal operating environments, or universal speculative currencies in its first horizon.

---

## Article 2: Human Agency and Voluntary Association

> Participation in Anarchadia is strictly voluntary. No physical resource, shelter, food, or democratic participation in face-to-face assemblies shall be conditioned upon the usage of digital tools. Every individual and cooperative retains the right of complete digital exit, the cryptographic destruction of personal relational metadata, and the right to fork both software and historical communal databases.

---

## Article 3: Membership, Identity, Pseudonymity, and Recovery

> Identity in Anarchadia is based on social recognition, formalized cryptographically via local, peer-vouched credentials. All personal data is private by default, utilizing Zero-Knowledge Identity (ZK-ID) to allow members to prove qualifications (e.g., syndicate membership or voting eligibility) without disclosing their physical name or key history. Key recovery relies on offline social secret sharing among trusted neighbors, rejecting any central register.

---

## Article 4: Rights of Persons and Duties of Institutions

> Every user of Anarchadia possesses equal, non-waivable, and cryptographically protected rights to: absolute privacy of personal life, complete pseudonymity in assembly voting, clear and non-technical explanations of any automated recommendation, permanent and visible record of their dissent, complete data portability, and the right of complete exit and fork. No community institution shall withhold survival resources during any active dispute or assembly appeal.

---

## Article 5: Transparency, Privacy, and the Commons

> Anarchadia operates on the foundational principle: Private by default for people, accountable by default for institutions. All individual communication, transaction histories, and consumption choices are cryptographically shielded using blind signatures and local-only storage. Conversely, all cooperative inventories, resource flows, institutional decisions, and steward resource allocations are 100% transparent, auditable, and un-redactable.

---

## Article 6: Governance Primitives

> Anarchadia provides a verified, modular library of governance primitives (Majority, Supermajority, Sortition, Quadratic Voting, Liquid Delegation, and Recall). All governance smart contracts must be formally verified using TLA+ against deadlock and exploit. All delegation is instantly revocable, all voting is pseudonymous via ZK-credentials, and all developer/founder weight is strictly non-privileged.

---

## Article 7: Delegation, Mandates, Administration, and Recall

> All administrative authority within Anarchadia is strictly narrow, temporary, and recallable. Administrative roles are treated as tasks with explicit boundaries, never as permanent ranks. All coordinating credentials and keys expire automatically at the end of their defined mandate, with developer and founder credentials expiring within 90 days unless renewed by the assembly. Immediate recall votes are cryptographically enforced via automatic smart-contract key disabling.

---

## Article 8: Conflict, Harm, Safety, and Restorative Process

> Anarchadia rejects all carceral, punitive, and police-based justice structures. It provides digital primitives to charter voluntary restorative justice circles and peer-arbitration. All mediation records are encrypted in local-only vaults. Members have an absolute right to demand immediate protective separation, and all automated, algorithmic, or machine-enforced punitive measures or resource deprivations are strictly prohibited.

---

## Article 9: Economic Coordination and Resource Stewardship

> Anarchadia supports plural, community-defined economic coordination models (including gifts, shared inventories, mutual credit, and labor-credit systems). It explicitly rejects the imposition of a single, universal currency. Basic physical necessities (food, water, medicine, housing) are guaranteed to all members by need, decoupled from credit balances. Care, emotional, and domestic labor are recognized as equal in value to physical production, and all developer allocations are subject to direct assembly review.

---

## Article 10: Ecological Responsibility

> Anarchadia integrates ecological limits into all collective planning processes. It tracks resource use and environmental costs strictly at the level of collective syndicates and cooperatives, explicitly prohibiting the tracking of personal individual footprints. Upstream and adjacent communities hold cryptographically signed rights to challenge production plans. The software is barred from executing automated blocks, and is restricted to warning and escalating environmental breaches to physical assemblies.

---

## Article 11: Epistemic Commons and Media Interpretation

> Anarchadia supports a decentralized, user-configured Epistemic Commons. The first MVP is strictly limited to decentralized feed sharing (via RSS/markdown over GossipAid), explicitly deferring automated SLM-based parsing and deprogramming to future horizons to prevent model bias and battery drain. User reading habits, feed lists, and library contents are encrypted locally. Future AI integrations must run 100% locally with open prompts, separating fact from value.

---

## Article 12: Technical Sovereignty and Architecture

> Anarchadia's architecture is local-first, offline-first, and peer-to-peer. The system must compile to run 100% local-only on a single device as its baseline operating condition. To support scale, communities may run replaceable, federated, and assembly-owned local servers and relays. All data stored on these shared relays must be cryptographically end-to-end encrypted, ensuring server operators cannot read private user data or relational metadata.

---

## Article 13: Security and Threat Model

> Anarchadia's security model is designed to withstand state-level surveillance, physical device seizure, domestic surveillance, and administrative abuse. All local storage is encrypted on-device. All mesh transit is encrypted peer-to-peer with traffic camouflage. Core modules must compile on older 32-bit hardware. Plausible deniability profiles via duress-PINs are built into the core lock module. Under no circumstances shall developer backdoors or master keys exist.

---

## Article 14: Ecosystem Boundaries

> Anarchadia maintains strict, non-negotiable boundaries with its sibling systems, Cerbanimo (work) and AUREA (reflection). Sibling applications must compile as entirely independent executable packages utilizing separate cryptographic keys and isolated local databases. Sibling systems may exchange signed capability events (e.g., mandate.granted, work.completed) over a modular, assembly-authorized bridge. Any silent merging of databases, profiles, accounts, or authority is a constitutional violation.

---

## Article 15: Federation, Subsidiarity, Exit, and Fork

> Anarchadia operates on strict bottom-up subsidiarity and federated communalism. Absolute authority resides permanently in local assemblies. Higher-level councils exist solely for coordinating tasks delegated upward, with delegates bound by cryptographic instructions and instantly recallable. Regional councils are strictly barred from levying non-consensual taxes or seizing local resources. Every community retains the absolute, non-negotiable right to leave the federation, carry its local data slice, and fork the software and database.

---

## Article 16: First Useful Community

> Anarchadia designates the 'Worker Cooperative / Syndicate' as its primary physical wedge for the Horizon 1 MVP cellular application. The system's design and testing will be evaluated strictly against five core journeys: Group Chartering, Membership Vouching, Proposal Creation, Decision Logging, and Ledger Export. Rotating operational roles may be delegated to recallable stewards, and all local databases must incorporate a one-click emergency wipe.

---

## Article 17: MVP Constitution

> Anarchadia's Horizon 1 MVP is constituted as a standard, user-space cross-platform cellular application. The MVP's scope is strictly limited to supporting direct-democratic coordination for local, physical worker cooperatives. It implements five core journeys: Group Chartering, Membership Roster, Proposal Creation, Decision Logging, and Ledger Export, running offline via direct Wi-Fi and Bluetooth node-to-node synchronization. All forms of currency, regional liquid voting, custom mesh gateway firmware, and bare-metal hypervisors are designated as explicit non-goals for this horizon.

---

## Article 18: Amendment, Experimentation, and Ratification

> This provisional constitution is a design recommendation only and holds no democratic legitimacy. Every article in this document is provisional and will sunset within 12 months unless explicitly ratified by real human assemblies in practice. All human ratification votes must utilize zero-knowledge voting ciphers to protect voter anonymity. Local assemblies hold the absolute, non-negotiable right to reject any provisional article, run custom local constitutional patches, and fork the software and database at any time. Developer overrides are strictly prohibited.
