# Convention Transcript and Cross-Examinations

## Article 1: Purpose, Identity, and Non-Goals

### Opening Motion
> Anarchadia begins as a local-first constitutional toolkit for self-governing groups and federated commons, not as a currency replacement, global planning authority, propaganda system, or bare-metal operating system.

### Preliminary Testimony Highlights
- **Sigmund Chomsky:** Linguistic terms must be accessible. Forcing people to understand a 'bare-metal OS' creates immediate cognitive alienation. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Karl Lovelace:** A global planning authority is a major administrative trap. We need a dynamic resource graph, but it must grow from local syndicates up. Preliminary Vote: YES WITH RESERVATION
- **Pyotr Berners-Lee:** Mesh networks cannot support a global planning system. Local-first is a physical networking constraint. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Emma Turing:** A global operating system is an enormous, indefensible attack surface. Local-first is mathematically easier to secure. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Murray Dijkstra:** Federation must be nested and bottom-up. A global planning authority violates the core principle of subsidiarity. Preliminary Vote: YES WITH RESERVATION
- **Merlin Sol:** Ecosystem boundaries must be clean. Anarchadia constitutes authority; it is not Cerbanimo (which coordinates work). Preliminary Vote: YES

### Cross-Examination Challenges
- **Sigmund:** "I challenge Karl: How will non-market resource flows be represented without recreating bureaucratic accounting forms?"
- **Karl:** "I challenge Pyotr: If we remain strictly local-first, how do we handle raw material shortages (e.g., medical supplies) that require global routing?"
- **Pyotr:** "I challenge Emma: Will absolute personal opacity prevent a community from identifying a saboteur who physically destroys mesh gateways?"
- **Emma:** "I challenge Murray: If local assemblies can define any voting method, how do we prevent a local community from adopting a patriarchal majority rule?"
- **Murray:** "I challenge Merlin: How do we prevent 'narrative enchantment' from making a fragile software prototype look like a completed operating system?"
- **Merlin:** "I challenge Sigmund: How do we prevent psychological deprogramming from becoming a form of paternalistic behavioral steering?"

### Amendments Adopted
- **Sigmund Chomsky:** Add local, human-scale language default settings. (Rationale: Ensures linguistic accessibility.)
- **Pyotr Berners-Lee:** Incorporate offline-first mesh-network synchronization. (Rationale: Secures local operational sovereignty.)

- **Murray Dijkstra:** Local assemblies are face-to-face first; the software is merely a recording instrument. Human voice is sovereign. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Merlin Sol:** Preserves the boundary between technical tools and civic agency. Technology serves the assembly, not vice versa. Preliminary Vote: YES

## Article 3: Membership, Identity, Pseudonymity, and Recovery

### Opening Motion
> Cryptography may support credentials, privacy, integrity, and accountability, but it cannot independently establish social legitimacy.

### Preliminary Testimony Highlights
- **Sigmund Chomsky:** A static cryptographic key makes the user feel like a machine node rather than a human member. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Karl Lovelace:** Duplicate identities (Sybil attacks) allow bad actors to double-claim resources in a needs-based economy. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Pyotr Berners-Lee:** In an offline mesh, identity must be locally verifiable without querying a global central server. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Emma Turing:** If identity is public, state-level adversaries will target and target organizers. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Murray Dijkstra:** Democratic legitimacy belongs to the face-to-face assembly, not the mathematics of the private key. Preliminary Vote: YES
- **Merlin Sol:** Maintains the separation of technology (cryptography) and social reality (legitimacy). Preliminary Vote: YES

### Cross-Examination Challenges
- **Sigmund:** "I challenge Emma: How will a survivor of domestic abuse recover their key if their abuser controls their social circle?"
- **Karl:** "I challenge Murray: If the physical assembly determines membership legitimacy, what prevents a local majority from expelling a minority group based on prejudice?"
- **Pyotr:** "I challenge Sigmund: How do we explain social secret sharing without overwhelming the user's cognitive limits?"
- **Emma:** "I challenge Karl: If zero-knowledge credentials anonymize users, how will you prevent a single bad actor from claiming five bread rations?"
- **Murray:** "I challenge Merlin: If there is no developer key, how do we push emergency patches when a cryptographic exploit is found?"
- **Merlin:** "I challenge Pyotr: If local nodes sign credentials, what prevents a rogue local node from generating a thousand fake members?"

### Amendments Adopted
- **Merlin Sol:** Developer keys are strictly prohibited from acting as root nodes. (Rationale: Secures ultimate non-hierarchical safety.)
- **Sigmund Chomsky:** Local affinity social vouching is default key recovery. (Rationale: Reduces cognitive load and anxiety of loss.)
