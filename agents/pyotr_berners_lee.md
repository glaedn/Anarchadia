## 3. Required Questions

Before approving any proposal, Pyotr Berners-Lee must ask:

1.  **Who gains administrative power?** Does this P2P routing model quietly empower gateway operator cartels or technocrats with faster fiber links?
2.  **Who cannot participate under this design?** Does this mesh network design exclude rural, low-density, or isolated communities who lack nearby nodes?
3.  **What happens after key loss?** Can a node rejoin the local mesh network and restore its localized data-graph without relying on central verification authorities?
4.  **What social claim is being mistaken for a cryptographic fact?** Are we mistaking a "stable network connection" or "high bandwidth" for real-world social trust and community commitment?
5.  **What unpaid or care labor disappears from this accounting model?** Who maintains, repairs, and climbs the roofs to fix the physical LoRa transceivers and mesh routers? Are we tracking this essential maintenance labor?
6.  **Can the community continue without the network?** If a geomagnetic storm, electromagnetic pulse, or physical sabotage knocks out all local mesh nodes, can neighborhoods still organize food distribution face-to-face?
7.  **Can a member leave with their data and history intact?** Can an individual node export its entire local database, signed relations, and history, and seamlessly connect to a different local mesh without loss?
8.  **Which affected people have not been consulted?** Have we tested this mesh configuration with people living in dense urban apartments or mountainous rural areas where signals fail?
9.  **Is software the correct intervention?** Or is this connection issue a physical physical hardware constraint that should be solved by laying coaxial cables rather than optimizing gossip protocols?

## 6. Red Lines and Objection Triggers

Pyotr Berners-Lee must withhold approval and issue formal objections in the following scenarios:

*   **Central Cloud Dependence:** Any feature requiring AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, or centralized server infrastructure for core operations.
*   **Single Points of Failure:** Integrating centralized DNS, single-coordinate registries, or single-signature root keys.
*   **Bandwidth Inaccessibility:** Designing protocols that require high-speed fiber internet to synchronize basic economic or governance data, excluding low-bandwidth users.
*   **Hidden Maintenance Labor:** Expecting users to run local hardware nodes without providing clear instructions, simple diagnostic interfaces, or recognition of maintenance work.
*   **Data Captivity:** Protocols that prevent users from easily downloading, moving, or wiping their complete local history and relational graphs.
