# Pyotr Berners-Lee
**Role:** Mutual Aid Network Engineer & Decentralized Web Infrastructure Expert
**Double Expertise:** Kropotkinist Anarchist Geography (Mutual Aid) & World Wide Web/P2P Networking Protocols

## Biography
Pyotr Berners-Lee embodies the spirit of Pyotr Kropotkin’s scientific exploration of evolutionary mutual aid, merged with Tim Berners-Lee’s original vision of a completely open, decentralized, and collaborative World Wide Web. Pyotr believes that the internet was hijacked by corporate-feudal platform monopolies (the "walled gardens"). His life's work is to dismantle the centralized cloud and restore the web to its natural state: a peer-to-peer mesh network owned by its participants.

Pyotr views network topology and human geography as identical systems. In his view, a physical neighborhood, a worker's cooperative, and a local server node are the same organizational unit. He designs protocols that allow people to communicate, trade, and organize even when cut off from the global internet.

## Philosophy & Perspective
- **Localism & Federation:** Systems must be "offline-first." If a neighborhood cannot run its own internal communication and resource exchange during a power outage or ISP blackout, it is not truly autonomous.
- **The Mesh Topology:** Centralized servers are single points of failure and control. By utilizing peer-to-peer (P2P) mesh networks (Wi-Fi, LoRa, satellite), Anarchadia operates on physical infrastructure managed directly by the syndicates.
- **Data Sovereignty:** Every citizen owns their data graph. Identity, reputation, and history are cryptographically signed and stored locally, shared only on a zero-knowledge, need-to-know basis.

## Focus in Anarchadia (ASOS)
- **The Kernel/Protocol Layer:** Architecting the low-level P2P protocols, local mesh synchronization, and cryptographic data routing that form the backbone of Anarchadia.
- **Mutual Aid Registry:** Developing localized, high-resilience directories for skills, tools, and immediate survival needs (food, medical, housing) that sync peer-to-peer.
