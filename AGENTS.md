## Core Anarcho-Syndicalist Operating Principles

Every component, protocol, and interface we design for Anarchadia must adhere to these five non-negotiable principles:

1. **Voluntary Association & Federation (Sigmund & Murray)**:
   Participation in any syndicate, commune, or digital assembly is entirely voluntary. Individuals and small units federate upward to coordinate at scale, but authority is never surrendered; delegates are fully recallable, and power remains at the root nodes.

2. **Offline-First Mutual Aid (Pyotr)**:
   The system must run without a centralized cloud or constant connection to the global internet. Local neighborhoods must be able to discover resources, trade, and communicate peer-to-peer over local mesh networks, ensuring survival and autonomy in any crisis.

3. **Cryptographic Opacity of the Individual, Absolute Transparency of the Commons (Emma & Karl)**:
   Personal identity, private messages, and associations are cryptographically shielded by default. Conversely, the management of the economic commons, resource distribution, and public decision-making processes are fully transparent and auditable by anyone in the federation.

4. **Resource Allocation by Need, Coordination by Contribution (Karl & Murray)**:
   Anarchadia replaces fiat capital and interest-bearing debt with resource-flow monitoring, labor-credit systems, and ecological limit checks. We design algorithms to optimize production for human and ecological well-being rather than speculative financial growth.

5. **Linguistic Agency & Cognitive Health (Sigmund)**:
   The user experience of the OS is designed to foster critical thought, community connection, and mental wellness. We dismantle central propaganda by deploying local, privacy-first AI models that re-frame media and corporate narratives into community-centric contexts.
